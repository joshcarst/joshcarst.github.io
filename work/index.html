The Shootout

The culmination of a semester's worth of effort, from sourcing cameras to laboring over the accompanying 56-page technical paper. Me and my two friends Andrea and Megan conducted our first cinema camera shootout between RED's HELIUM 8K and Blackmagic Design's Ursa Mini Pro G2 4.6K to determine whether all those extra pixels are necessary.

Barely Competently Explaining Phase Dispersion Data Embedding

For my image processing & computer vision class, I was tasked with explaining a cryptography-related algorithm of my choice via either paper or video. I opted for a video on data embedding via phase dispersion and I wanted it to stand out. A relatively simple process, but will the wind cooperate?

Somewhat Competently Explaining Seam Carving

A follow up to the Phase Dispersion video above, this time I pick up my $15 Ebay camcorder and hand it off to my housemate to whom I then explain seam carving, a popular image resizing algorithm found in Photoshop among other applications. Once again a relatively simple process, but will she have any idea what I'm talking about?

Carstens - ALMOST

In addition to pursuing visual arts and sciences, I also enjoy working on music and audio-related projects. My most recent album, ALMOST, is a personal exploration of the themes of longing and loneliness that have come and gone through college. I produced and mixed the entire album myself in Ableton as well as recorded my own vocals, along with those of friends and family. I also produced two accompanying music videos utilizing virtual production techniques, and designed a large amount of promotional imagery for physical mediums around campus.

Peppermint

An extended cover I made with my cousins for Jack Stauber's Peppermint because we felt the original was too short. For this project I completely reproduced the original instrumental from scratch as well as originally orchestrating everything beyond the first 30 seconds. I then recorded and mixed my cousins' vocals to give them that 90s VHS sound. Additionally, I shot and edited the accompanying music video.

Freshman Imaging Project - small object 3D imaging array

Along with a team of other Motion Picture and Imaging Science freshmen, we designed an imaging system from scratch used to assist the Seneca Park Zoo with automatically capturing 3D models of unknown species of insects from Madagascar. In particular, I made crucial decisions involving the camera modules and lenses used on the final array, as well as writing the script to run our image output through modelling software. As fruits of our labor, we were featured on News 8 WROC and at Imagine RIT 2019 as exhibitors.
